The City of Nablus

The city of Nablus is distinguished by an embroidery heritage that blends traditional Palestinian character with Levantine influences, due to its commercial location and historical openness to Damascus and the Levant. This blend is evident in the colors, stitches, and symbols of the embroidery, combining simplicity, elegance, and precision of detail.

Features of Nablus Embroidery

① Colors

  • Nablus embroidery primarily relies on deep red and dark green, the colors most associated with the city.
  • Linen and silk threads are clearly used, a result of the nature of the old Nablus markets.
  • Black or navy blue may appear as background colors, giving the embroidery clarity and elegance.

② Stitches

  • Nablus relies on the Filé (Cross) stitch, which is the basic Palestinian agricultural stitch.
  • The Running Stitch is also used to outline external lines.
  • Some old pieces contain touches of Couching stitch, especially when using thick silk threads.
  • Nablus embroidery is less dense than that of Hebron, but more precise and symmetrical in the distribution of decorations.

③ Common Symbols

  • Grape Leaf: Long, parallel lines resembling branches.
  • Pomegranate Flower: Symbolizes fertility and goodness.
  • Geometric Diamonds: Express protection and strength.
  • Olive Branch: A symbol of peace and belonging to Palestine.
  • Parallel Lines: Indicate harmony and stability in social life.
