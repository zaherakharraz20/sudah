A Thread That Unites Us… An Identity That Speaks for Us

This project is the result of the efforts of four students, brought together by the desire to transform what we learn inside university classrooms into work that carries a meaning greater than just an academic exercise. We chose Palestinian embroidery specifically because it is not merely a beautiful visual art, but a national language that preserves the memory of the land and tells the stories of Palestinian cities and villages for hundreds of years. Our work began with a deep understanding of the symbols, stitches, and colors that distinguish each region, then we connected this theoretical knowledge with modern design techniques to present work that reflects our heritage in a contemporary style. Through this project, we discovered how science can become a tool for protecting cultural heritage, and how we, as students, can use our skills in research, analysis, and creativity to serve a national cause that touches our identity. This project was more than just a university assignment for us—it was a space that strengthened our sense of belonging and deepened our appreciation for embroidery as one of the most important symbols of Palestinian resilience. We hope that our work reflects part of the beauty of this heritage and contributes in its own way to preserving it and passing it on to future generations in a manner worthy of its rich legacy.
